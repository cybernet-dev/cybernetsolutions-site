# Momentum FAQ

## Why does Momentum limit active Momentum Areas?

Momentum is designed around one simple idea: lasting change comes from focused, consistent action.

Research on habit formation has shown that habits are most likely to develop through repeated performance in a stable context. Completing a behavior regularly and under consistent conditions helps strengthen the cue-response associations that eventually make the behavior feel more automatic.

Research on cognitive load and goal management also suggests that actively pursuing too many behavior changes at once can divide attention, increase mental effort, and make it harder to stay consistent. As the number of active goals grows, each one competes for your time, attention, and motivation.

To encourage meaningful progress, Momentum intentionally limits the number of active Momentum Areas. This helps you focus on what matters most today while building the consistency needed for long-term habit formation.

You can always change your active areas as your priorities evolve. Momentum's goal isn't to help you do more; it's to help you build habits that last.
